Detailed Description

The Basin-Hopping planner.

This algorithm follows a random restart procedure:

  • each round starts from a randomly sampled conformation (produced by a "sampler"). Each round builds a trajectory of minima, by performing a succession of greedy local searches. During the construction of the trajectory, each greedy local search starts from:
  • either the random sample, in the case of the first step of the trajectory
  • or a conformation obtained by perturbing the minimum obtained at the previous step (and produced by a "global perturber": large perturbation of a single phi or psi dihedral). A greedy local search is a succession of iterative perturbations performed by a "local perturber" (small perturbation of a single dihedral). Each greedy local search goes on while the energy decreases or the Metropolis criterion is satisfied, until a given maximum number of consecutive failures is reached. Each greedy local search produces a minimum over the energy landscape. Each trajectory produces a milestone: the minimum having the lowest energy; this corresponds to a variant of BH: the Monotonic Basin Hopping.
