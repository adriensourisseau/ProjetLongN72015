Detailed Description

Compute the energy of a molecular system using the AMBER force field.

This Energy Computer re-implements AMBER's force field without computing the energy associated with bond lengths and bond angles and with the following solvent parameters: igb = 0 or 1, gbsa = 0.

The different components of the energy of the molecular system are calculated (or not) as follows:

  • Some close-range interactions between atoms (namely interactions of types 1-3, 2-3 and 2-4 in dihedrals) are ignored because they are related to bond lengths and bond angles.
  • The non-bonded energy due to close-range interactions of type 1-4 is computed (it corresponds to the sum of the terms 1-4 EEL and 1-4 VDW in AMBER).
  • The 'dihedral energy' is the torsion energy of dihedrals (DIHED in AMBER).
  • Long-range interactions between atoms are computed using the interactingAtoms adjacency list: the two computed terms are 'electrostatic energy' and (potential) 'van der Waals energy' (EEL and VDWAALS in AMBER).
  • Finally, the 'solvent energy' corresponds to electrostatic and van der Waals terms between the solvent molecules and the molecular system (EGB in AMBER).

N.B.: Here "long-range" means: concerning atoms that are far apart in the sequence (but not necessarily in space). "Close-range" means: concerning atoms that belong to the same dihedral.

N.B.: Here the interactingAtoms are stored as a "triangular" adjacency list, to avoid computing twice the energy due to a given pair of atoms.
